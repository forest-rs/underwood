# Charter-000: Spearhead, proof, and stewardship

- **Status:** Open — human ratification required
- **Bead:** `und-oh0.11.1`
- **Authority:** `UNDERWOOD_HANDOVER.md` §§6.3–6.4, 28, 29, 35.1

## Goal

Ratify the institutional conditions under which Underwood can pursue its
complete architecture without confusing breadth, activity, or presentation
with progress.

## Non-goals

- Choosing technical representations owned by ADRs.
- Making compatibility promises.
- Creating a private correctness tier.
- Treating a funding recommendation as an accomplished institution.

## Fence

This charter owns the flagship, proof language, active-work discipline, and
stewardship commitments; it explicitly does not own subsystem architecture or
commercial product implementation.

## Proposed declaration

The starting recommendation, not yet ratified, is:

1. The living agent document is the flagship and first Product-proven client.
2. The living design specification, million-line technical editor, and
   multilingual compositor remain standing wind tunnels.
3. Product and wind tunnels use public Underwood contracts.
4. One cross-cutting capability campaign receives active product pressure.
5. Proof statuses are literal and backed by checked-in evidence.
6. Engine crates, ordinary adapters, formats, and conformance corpora remain
   Apache-2.0 OR MIT common infrastructure.
7. A funded flagship product and services may support a permanent
   text/host/accessibility/conformance team without withholding a private
   correctness tier.
8. Underwood remains headless, independently adoptable, and useful without
   Overstory.

## Alternatives requiring consideration

### Infrastructure-first commons

Build Underwood primarily as reusable infrastructure and wait for downstream
products to supply adoption pressure. This preserves neutrality but risks a
large surface with no sustained product tyrant or conformance funding.

### Funded commons anchored by the flagship

Use the living agent document to create adoption and fund shared conformance
while keeping the engine and evidence common. This is the handover's
recommendation.

### Closed product with an extracted core

Prioritize a product and release reusable pieces later. This concentrates
pressure but makes private shortcuts and a private correctness tier much more
likely.

## Ratification questions

The human decision must explicitly answer:

- Is the living agent document the flagship?
- Are the four proof statuses plus Product-proven binding project language?
- Is one active cross-cutting campaign the default?
- Which artifacts are irrevocably common infrastructure?
- What may fund the commons without creating a private correctness tier?
- Who initially owns conformance, Unicode, accessibility, security, and host
  maintenance?
- How do external contributors gain durable ownership?

## Evidence required before ratification

- A capability map showing active, gated, and dormant work.
- The proof ledger and validator running locally and in CI.
- A public-API-only rule represented in review templates.
- An initial maintenance and funding statement, even if modest.
- A clear separation between Underwood engine ownership and Overstory product
  ownership.
