# Charter-000: Spearhead, proof, and stewardship

- **Status:** Accepted
- **Accepted:** 2026-07-21 by Bruce Mitchener
- **Bead:** `und-oh0.11.1`
- **Authority:** `UNDERWOOD_HANDOVER.md` §§6.3–6.4, 28, 29, 35.1

## Goal

Ratify the institutional conditions under which Underwood can pursue its
complete architecture without confusing breadth, activity, or presentation
with progress.

## Non-goals

- Choosing technical representations owned by ADRs.
- Making compatibility promises.
- Creating a private correctness tier.
- Treating a funding recommendation as an accomplished institution.

## Fence

This charter owns the flagship, proof language, active-work discipline, and
stewardship commitments; it explicitly does not own subsystem architecture or
commercial product implementation.

## Ratified declaration

1. The living agent document is the flagship and first Product-proven client.
2. The living design specification, million-line technical editor, and
   multilingual compositor remain standing wind tunnels.
3. Product and wind tunnels use public Underwood contracts.
4. One cross-cutting capability campaign receives active product pressure.
5. Proof statuses are literal and backed by checked-in evidence.
6. Engine crates, ordinary adapters, formats, and conformance corpora remain
   Apache-2.0 OR MIT common infrastructure.
7. A funded flagship product and services may support a permanent
   text/host/accessibility/conformance team without withholding a private
   correctness tier.
8. Underwood remains headless, independently adoptable, and useful without
   Overstory.

## Ratification answers

### Flagship and proof language

- The living agent document is the flagship and first intended
  Product-proven client.
- `Specified`, `Executable`, `Measured`, `Conformant`, and `Product-proven` are
  binding project language. A capability occupies exactly one proof stage at a
  time.
- One active cross-cutting capability campaign is the default. A second active
  campaign requires a recorded dependency, capacity owner, and explicit
  sequencing decision; ordinary review and maintenance do not count as a
  product campaign.

### Common infrastructure boundary

The following are common infrastructure and remain Apache-2.0 OR MIT:

- Underwood engine and core crates;
- ordinary adapters, including the maintained Parley and collaboration
  adapters;
- stable interchange formats, schemas, and protocol definitions;
- conformance corpora, trace generators, expected results, proof ledgers, and
  validators;
- public-API wind tunnels, benchmarks, examples, and compatibility fixtures;
- documentation required to implement, validate, or independently adopt the
  public contracts.

A product may own private workflows, product policy, hosted services, and
business data. It may not depend on an unreleased engine fix, private corpus,
private data bundle, or private correctness path to pass a public Underwood
claim.

### Funding statement

No recurring external funding or permanent maintenance budget is represented
as secured at ratification. That is an explicit starting condition, not a
sustainability claim.

The approved model may fund the commons through a flagship product, support and
integration services, sponsorship, grants, or shared industry investment.
Funding may prioritize public work but may not purchase a private correctness
tier or delay a generally required engine fix from the common infrastructure.
Product revenue and common-infrastructure licensing remain separate decisions.

### Initial ownership

Bruce Mitchener is the bootstrap project steward and decision authority. The
bootstrap steward owns sequencing and the obligation to appoint or recruit
domain owners; this does not pretend that one person already supplies every
specialist maintenance function.

| Domain | Initial accountable state | Activation gate |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Project sequencing and architecture gates | Bruce Mitchener | Active |
| Conformance institution | Bootstrap steward pending a named conformance owner | Cannot claim Conformant without appointment |
| Unicode and international text data | Unfilled specialist ownership | No full-support claim or bundle stabilization |
| Accessibility | Unfilled specialist ownership | No end-to-end accessibility claim |
| Security and adversarial-resource policy | Bootstrap steward for triage; specialist review unfilled | No hardened-input claim |
| Host bridges and platform maintenance | Unfilled per-platform ownership | No platform Product-proven claim |

An unfilled role is visible in the proof ledger and blocks the corresponding
claim. It does not authorize silent de-scoping.

### External contributor path

Durable ownership is earned through:

1. accepted work and review in one declared fence;
2. maintenance of its tests, conformance evidence, and migration obligations
   across more than one change;
3. demonstrated review judgment and timely issue stewardship;
4. nomination by an existing steward and a recorded ownership decision;
5. repository permissions and review routing that match the recorded scope.

Ownership is scoped, reviewable, and revocable for inactivity or repeated
breach of the constitution. Employment or funding may provide time; it does not
skip the evidence path.

### Underwood and Overstory

Underwood owns the headless semantic, preparation, editing, layout, scene,
accessibility, and conformance contracts. Overstory owns flagship product
experience, host composition, product-specific policy, and business concerns.
The flagship exercises Underwood only through contracts available to an
independent adopter. Product pressure may change Underwood priorities but not
smuggle Overstory types into its core.

## Alternatives requiring consideration

### Infrastructure-first commons

Build Underwood primarily as reusable infrastructure and wait for downstream
products to supply adoption pressure. This preserves neutrality but risks a
large surface with no sustained product tyrant or conformance funding.

### Funded commons anchored by the flagship

Use the living agent document to create adoption and fund shared conformance
while keeping the engine and evidence common. This is the handover's
recommendation.

### Closed product with an extracted core

Prioritize a product and release reusable pieces later. This concentrates
pressure but makes private shortcuts and a private correctness tier much more
likely.

## Evidence required before ratification

- A capability map showing active, gated, and dormant work.
- The proof ledger and validator running locally and in CI.
- A public-API-only rule represented in review templates.
- An initial maintenance and funding statement, even if modest.
- A clear separation between Underwood engine ownership and Overstory product
  ownership.

At ratification, the capability map, proof ledger, local validator, and review
template exist and pass locally. The GitHub workflow is checked in but has not
run on an authoritative remote. The public `forest-rs/underwood` origin exists,
but it has no branch, required checks, or ruleset until the initial history is
explicitly published. `und-oh0.11.2` owns that remaining proof. The charter's
acceptance does not mislabel local executable governance as remote-green
conformance.

## Decision

Accepted on 2026-07-21. Underwood adopts the funded-commons model anchored by
the living agent document, the binding proof vocabulary, the single-campaign
default, the common-infrastructure boundary, the honest initial ownership and
funding state, and the contributor path above.

## Consequences

The first private foundation campaign may proceed once the mandatory-decision
gate records all five accepted decisions. Product and proof claims remain
limited by actual owners and evidence. Missing funding, remote controls, or
specialist ownership stays visible and blocks the corresponding claim rather
than shrinking the architecture.
